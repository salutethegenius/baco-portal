_This document provides an overview of key processing activities for the BACO Portal. It should be reviewed and updated at least annually._

## 1. Membership management

- **Purpose**: Create and manage BACO member accounts, maintain membership status and contact details.
- **Data subjects**: Members, applicants, certain employer representatives.
- **Data categories**:
  - Identification and contact details (name, email, phone, addresses).
  - Professional information (employer, job title, qualifications, years of experience).
  - Membership data (membership type, status, join date, annual fee, membership number).
- **Legal basis**:
  - Performance of contract / membership relationship.
  - Legitimate interests of BACO in operating the association.
- **Recipients**:
  - BACO officers and administrators.
  - Hosting and infrastructure providers as data processors.
- **Retention**:
  - Active membership + generally up to 6 years after last activity, subject to legal advice and business needs.
- **International transfers**:
  - Possible where cloud infrastructure is hosted outside The Bahamas; safeguarded via provider contracts and access controls.

## 2. Event management and CPD tracking

- **Purpose**: Organise and manage events, trainings and conferences; track attendance and CPD/CE credits.
- **Data subjects**: Members, non-member attendees, speakers.
- **Data categories**:
  - Registration details (name, email, contact information, organisation).
  - Event data (sessions attended, registration type).
  - Certificates and CPD hours (where applicable).
- **Legal basis**:
  - Performance of contract (event participation).
  - Legitimate interests (running and documenting events, CPD history).
- **Recipients**:
  - BACO officers.
  - Event service providers where necessary (e.g. venue organisers, virtual event tools).
- **Retention**:
  - Registration and attendance records retained for at least 6 years; certificates retained as long as professionally relevant or until a valid deletion request and no overriding legal basis exists.
- **International transfers**:
  - As above, where infrastructure or tools are hosted abroad.

## 3. Payments and invoicing

- **Purpose**: Process membership fees, event fees and other payments; issue invoices and receipts.
- **Data subjects**: Members, paying organisations, attendees.
- **Data categories**:
  - Contact and billing details.
  - Invoice details (amount, description, invoice number, status).
  - Payment metadata (payment method, status, reference IDs from payment processors).
  - BACO does **not** store full card numbers in the Portal.
- **Legal basis**:
  - Performance of contract.
  - Compliance with legal and financial record-keeping obligations.
- **Recipients**:
  - Payment service providers.
  - Accounting / audit providers where applicable.
- **Retention**:
  - Financial records retained for at least 7 years or as required by Bahamian law.
- **International transfers**:
  - Payment and email service providers may be located abroad; safeguarded via contracts and technical measures.

## 4. Document management

- **Purpose**: Allow members to upload and BACO to review professional documents (certificates, IDs, receipts, etc.).
- **Data subjects**: Members and, where relevant, individuals mentioned in documents.
- **Data categories**:
  - Uploaded files (PDFs and similar).
  - Metadata (filename, category, upload date, status, reviewer).
  - Limited sensitive data where documents include identification or other personal information.
- **Legal basis**:
  - Performance of contract (membership and CPD verification).
  - Legitimate interests (verifying eligibility, managing risk).
- **Recipients**:
  - BACO officers responsible for verification.
  - Secure file storage provider as data processor.
- **Retention**:
  - Kept as long as necessary to evidence membership and professional standing, subject to retention rules and member rights.
- **International transfers**:
  - Possible via cloud storage outside The Bahamas; safeguarded via access controls and contractual commitments.

## 5. Messaging and communications

- **Purpose**: Enable members and administrators to communicate securely within the Portal.
- **Data subjects**: Members and BACO officers.
- **Data categories**:
  - Message content, subject lines, timestamps, sender and recipient IDs.
- **Legal basis**:
  - Performance of contract (member support and communication).
  - Legitimate interests (efficient administration and record keeping).
- **Recipients**:
  - BACO officers and relevant members in each conversation.
  - Email service provider for notifications.
- **Retention**:
  - Messages retained while relevant to the membership relationship and for a reasonable period afterwards (e.g. up to 6 years), subject to legal or complaint-handling needs.
- **International transfers**:
  - Email providers and hosting may be outside The Bahamas.

## 6. Sensitive background and disciplinary information (where applicable)

- **Purpose**: Assess membership applications and manage professional conduct matters in line with BACO rules.
- **Data subjects**: Members and applicants.
- **Data categories**:
  - Information about professional misconduct findings or pending matters.
  - Information about criminal convictions where disclosed.
  - Regulatory registration information and related context.
- **Legal basis**:
  - Explicit consent from the data subject (through application forms).
  - Legitimate interests of BACO in protecting the integrity of the profession and membership body.
- **Recipients**:
  - Very limited group of authorised officers or committee members dealing with membership vetting or conduct.
- **Retention**:
  - Only for as long as necessary to make and document relevant decisions; periodic review required. Older records should be archived or anonymised where feasible.
- **International transfers**:
  - Minimised; only via secure infrastructure and strictly controlled access.

## 7. Logs, analytics and security monitoring

- **Purpose**: Maintain security, diagnose issues, and understand Portal usage patterns.
- **Data subjects**: All Portal users.
- **Data categories**:
  - Technical logs (IP address, timestamps, URLs, error messages).
  - Limited usage analytics (page views, feature usage), where enabled.
- **Legal basis**:
  - Legitimate interests (security, reliability, improvement of services).
- **Recipients**:
  - System administrators and service providers involved in monitoring and hosting.
- **Retention**:
  - Logs generally kept between 6 and 24 months, unless needed longer for investigations.
- **International transfers**:
  - As per hosting and monitoring provider locations.

---

For each new feature or processing activity, this inventory should be extended with:

- Purpose and description.
- Data subjects and categories.
- Legal basis.
- Recipients / processors.
- Retention period.
- Transfer and security considerations.
